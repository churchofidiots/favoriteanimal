My favorite animal is a Whale!

6/11/2025
By Namiko Aoyagi ☆　奈美子


Whales are a widely distributed and diverse group of fully aquatic placental marine mammals.
As an informal and colloquial grouping, they correspond to large members of the infraorder Cetacea,
i.e. all cetaceans apart from dolphins and porpoises.

  • Whales are mammals:Despite living in the ocean, whales are mammals, meaning they breathe air, have skin (not scales), are warm-blooded, give birth to live young, and feed their young with milk.
  • They can hold their breath for a very long time: Whales have evolved to hold their breath for extended periods, allowing them to dive deep in search of food.
  • Whales are incredibly large: Blue whales are the largest animals on Earth, reaching lengths of up to 100 feet and weighing around 200 tons.
  • They use different feeding strategies: Baleen whales strain krill and plankton with baleen plates, while toothed whales actively hunt and eat fish, squid, and other marine life.
  • Some whales are very social: Whales often travel and communicate in groups, forming strong social bonds.
  • Whale poop is a valuable nutrient source: Whale "pump" or "whale poop" helps move nutrients from the deep ocean to the surface, benefiting phytoplankton and the overall ocean ecosystem.
  • Whales migrate long distances: Gray whales undertake one of the longest migrations of any mammal, traveling thousands of miles to breeding grounds.
  • Whales can be very loud: The blue whale is known as one of the loudest animals on Earth, with vocalizations reaching 188 decibels.
  • Some whales have multiple stomachs: Certain whale species have a multi-chambered stomach, aiding in digestion of their prey.
  • Whales have long lifespans: Bowhead whales can live for up to 200 years, while orcas and humpbacks can live for over 100 and 80 years, respectively.
